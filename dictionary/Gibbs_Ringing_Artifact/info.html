Gibbs Ringing Artifact

Artifact Name

Gibbs Ringing Artifact

Modality

ASL MRI

Category

Reconstruction

Short Description

Gibbs ringing artifact occurs due to limited sampling in k-space during image reconstruction.

How It Appears in Image

Alternating bright and dark lines appear near sharp edges in the image.

Primary Cause

Truncation of high-frequency components during Fourier reconstruction.

Clinical Impact

Can mimic small structures or lesions, leading to misinterpretation.

Severity Level

Low

Common Fix

Increase spatial resolution or apply filtering during reconstruction.
